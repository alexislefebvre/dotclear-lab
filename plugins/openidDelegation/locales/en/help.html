OpenID is an open standard that allows users to authenticate to websites without having to create a new password. This plugin lets you use your blog address as the OpenID username, delegating the authentication requests to another provider.

You can create an account on a free OpenID provider, for example myOpenID, and delegate authentication. This way, if you want to change your provider, your OpenID username will stay the same: your blog's URL.

This delegation is done by adding headers to your homepage, and this plugin generates them.

Use OpenID delegation
General switch to enable or disable OpenID delegation.
Provider
Choose one of the preconfigured OpenID providers. If your provider is not in the list, you can choose "Custom" and paste the delegation instructions directly.
Username
You username on your OpenID provider, if it's preconfigured.
Headers
If your provider is not preconfigured, paste in this field the OpenID delegation instructions. They look like the following lines: 
         <link rel="openid.server" href="https://www.myopenid.com/server" />
         <link rel="openid.delegate" href="http://username.myopenid.com" />
